Statistical privacy

These are my notes of various textbook chapters and research papers in differential privacy. These notes reflect my attempts to understand the directions of research in statistical privacy. As a general guide for these notes: Definitions are always in blue , lemmas are written in orange and theorems are written in Green. Excerpts that provide intuition are displayed in purple and open questions and confusions are listed in red. The notes are divided into multiple views:

Individual research papers/mathematical tools to understand privacy

Sometimes I study a research paper or textbook chapter in isolation to understand its contents. Usually this is done to understand background material that will help me tackle a problem I care about. There is little big qpicture in those notes. In this notes I cannot always explain why someone not interested in differential privacy should care about these results.

N-gram extraction / private set union

Contribution Venue Authors
Differentially private set union : A datasets of n user, each with their own
set Wi ⊂ U, where U is a very large universe of items (possibly
unbounded). The basic laplace mechanism needs to much noise due to
high worst case sensitivity. They present an adaptive algorithm that keeps
sensitivity small for the laplace/gaussian mechanism. Once this is done, it’s just
the well understood additive noise mechanism.

Areas of improvement: (1) Algorithm is sequential and thus a federated version does not exist. (2) There are no utility guarantees presented. 		ICML 2020 Gopi, Gulhane, Kulkarni, Shen, Shokouhi, Yekhanin
Differentially private n gram
extraction: The setup is very similar as the previous problem. Now. instead of items users
have sequences with each element from a very large universe. They iteratively use set union,
to construct a private ngram extraction method.

Areas of improvement: (1) Cannot be distributed (2) How does McMahan and Grahams
trie heavy hitter algorithm compare to this. Once again it will allow us to compare sampling vs
additive noise. 		NIPS 2021 Kim, Gopi, Kulkarni, Yekhanin

Secure Comparison Protocols

Contribution Venue Authors
Secure aggregation for a single round: Under the honest but curious model, the server is able to compute
the sum of user inputs even in the case of t dropouts. The protocol only works for a single round of computing
sums. 		2017 ACM SIGSAC Conference on Computer and Communications Security
 Bonawitz, Keith and Ivanov, Vladimir and Kreuter, Ben and Marcedone, Antonio and McMahan, H Brendan and Patel, Sarvar and Ramage, Daniel and Segal, Aaron and Seth, Karn
Yao’s millionaire problem: Given two millionaires a and b, both parties with to compute the value of a < b without,
seeing each others values. A few ways to target this problem. One way is to convert the problem into private set intersection. Then use homomorphic encryption. Link 		International Conference on Applied Cryptography and Network Security(2005) Hsiao-Ying Lin and Wen-Guey Tzeng
Private set intersection: Given two sets A and B, both parties want to compute A intersection B without revealing A and B. One solution,
is to use an honest server, otherwise known as server aided PSI or third party PSI 		BLOG post Avishay Yanai
